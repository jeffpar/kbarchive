Q261078: Motocross Madness 2: Hangs When You Click "Start Race!"

Article: Q261078
Product(s): Microsoft Home Games
Version(s): 2.0
Operating System(s): 
Keyword(s): kb3rdparty kbdisplay kbimu msgame kbgraphxlinkcriticalkbfaq
Last Modified: 01-JUN-2001

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The information in this article applies to:

- Microsoft Motocross Madness 2, version 2.0 
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

SYMPTOMS
========

When you attempt to start a race in Microsoft Motocross Madness 2, you may
experience the following symptoms:

- Motocross Madness 2 may stop responding (hang).

- Textures on the motorcycle, the rider, or the ground may appear distorted.

CAUSE
=====

This behavior can occur if a Hercules Power Drive Terminator Beast video adapter
is installed in your computer.

RESOLUTION
==========

To resolve this issue, use the following methods in the order in which they are
presented.

Reduce the Display Resolution in the Game
-----------------------------------------

To reduce the display resolution in the game:

1. Start Motocross Madness 2, and then click Options ([GRAPHIC:the three gears])
  on the startup screen.

2. Click the Graphics tab.

3. In the Display Resolution box, click 640 X 480.

4. Click Back ([GRAPHIC:the pointing hand]) until you return to the startup
  screen.

Force Textures to Fit in Video Memory
-------------------------------------

To force textures to fit in video memory:

1. Start Motocross Madness 2, and then click Options ([GRAPHIC:the three gears])
  on the startup screen.

2. Click the Graphics tab.

3. Click Advanced.

4. Under Emergency Repair Features, click to select the "Force textures to fit
  in video memory" check box.

  NOTE: This speeds up the game, but reduces the quality of the graphics in the
  game.

5. Click Back ([GRAPHIC:the pointing hand]) until you return to the startup
  screen.

MORE INFORMATION
================

If the issue continues to occur, download and install the latest video driver
for your video adapter from the following Guillemot Web site:

  http://www.guillemot.com

To determine the brand and model of your video adapter:

1. Click Start, point to Settings, and then click Control Panel.

2. Double-click System.

3. Click Device Manager.

4. Double-click Display Adapters.

In the branch under Display Adapters, the brand and model of your video adapter
is displayed.

5. Close Control Panel.
